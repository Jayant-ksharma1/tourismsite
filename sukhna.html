Sukhna Lake

Sukhna Lake is an artificial reservoir at the foothills of the Himalayas, known for its serene beauty and recreational activities. It was created in 1958 by damming the Sukhna Choe stream, and now serves as a popular destination for boating, morning walks, and bird watching.

History

The lake was created by the Chandigarh administration, and its purpose was to provide water to the city and create an attractive recreation spot. Over the years, it has become one of the most well-known landmarks in Chandigarh.

Interesting Facts

  • The lake is spread across an area of 3 square kilometers.
  • Boating is a favorite activity here, with both paddle boats and shikaras available for hire.
  • It is home to various species of migratory birds, especially during the winter months.
