Hand Monument

The Hand Monument is a large sculpture located in the Capitol Complex. Created by Pierre Jeanneret, it stands as a symbol of peace, reconciliation, and the community's unity. The open hand is a prominent feature of Chandigarh's identity, serving as a symbol for the city's progressive and inclusive vision.

History

Designed by Pierre Jeanneret, the Hand Monument is one of the most iconic features of Chandigarh. It symbolizes the city’s ideals of peace and openness. The monument's unique design reflects the modernist ideals that shaped the city’s architecture and urban planning.

Interesting Facts

  • The monument stands tall at 26 feet and is made of concrete.
  • It was designed to rotate with the wind, symbolizing the city's openness to change.
  • It is one of the most recognized symbols of Chandigarh, representing the vision of Le Corbusier and his team.
