Rose Garden

The Zakir Hussain Rose Garden in Chandigarh is Asia's largest rose garden, home to over 1,600 different species of roses. The garden is spread across 30 acres of land and also houses a variety of other plants, including medicinal herbs and trees.

History

The garden was developed in 1967 by Dr. M.S. Randhawa, the first Chief Commissioner of Chandigarh, and is named after Zakir Hussain, the former President of India.

Interesting Facts

  • The garden features over 1,200 varieties of roses, making it one of the largest rose gardens in Asia.
  • Every year, the Rose Festival is held here, attracting thousands of visitors.
  • Along with roses, the garden also has several species of trees and medicinal plants.
